Red Pony: Equipment

Red Pony is an American pale ale. Here's everything you'll need to make it:

  • Primary and secondary fermentation vessels
  • Bung, airlock and tap
  • Hydrometer and measuring cylinder
  • Stirring spoon and bottle filler
  • Young's American pale ale malt extract
  • Brewers' yeast
  • Priming sugar
  • 40 Pint bottles, crowns and a capper

Red Pony: Step one

Sterilise the fermenter, lid, stirrer, bung, airlock, tap and any washers for at least ten minutes. Rinse thoroughly.


Pour the malt extract into the fermentation and add three litres of boiling water. Stir until dissolved, then add the brewing sugar and stir again.

Red Pony: Step two

Add 23 litres of cold water and stir the wort thoroughly. Once everything is well-mixed, stir vigorously for three mintues to oxygenate the wort. Extract some of the wort to take a specific gravity reading.


When the temperature of the wort has dropped below 25 degrees C, sprinkle the yeast on the surface, gentle stir, and then fit the lid and airlock.

Red Pony: Step three

Leave the brew to ferment for around 15 days at 20-24 degrees C. Regularly check the airlock to ensure the brew is fermenting.


Fermentation will take longer at cooler temperatures. Fit the heating belt if required.

Red Pony: Step four

When bubbles escape the airlock at a rate lower than one per minute, take a specific gravity reading. If it is below 1.010 then add the hops. If it is higher than 1.010, then wait a few more days.


When adding the hops, gently scatter them on the surface of the wort. Do not stir the wort. Replace the lid and leave for two to three days.

Red Pony: Step five

Take a specific gravity reading. If it has changed, then wait another day or two. If it is stable, then syphon the beer into the sterilised secondary fermentation vessel.


Be careful not to disturb the sediment.


The final specific gravity reading should be below 1.008.

Red Pony: Step six

Add the priming sugar to the vessel and stir well before bottling, using the bottle filler.


Fit the crowns to the bottles and record the date. Store them upright in a warm place for two weeks before transferring them to the bunker for a further month.


Enjoy your Red Pony!
