Mud: Equipment

Mud is a nut-brown ale. Here's everything you'll need to make it:

  • Primary and secondary fermentation vessels
  • Bung, airlock and tap
  • Hydrometer and measuring cylinder
  • Stirring spoon and bottle filler
  • Munton's Connoiseurs' malt extract
  • Brewing sugar or Spraymalt
  • Brewers' yeast
  • 40 Pint bottles, crowns and a capper

Mud: Step one

Sterilise the fermenter, lid, stirrer, bung, airlock, tap and any washers for at least ten minutes. Rinse thoroughly.


Stand malt extrack in hot water for five minutes so it softens. Boil six pints (3.5 litres) of water.

Mud: Step two

Pour the malt extract into the primary fermentation vessel.


Add the six pints of boiling water and 1kg of brewing sugar or suitable substitue. Mix thoroughly.

Mud: Step three

Add 31 pints (17.5 litres) of cold water and stir throughly to oxygenate the wort. Sprinkle the yeast onto the surface but do not stir.


Cover the vessel, fit the airlock and leave to ferment for four to six days at 18-21 degrees C. Use the heating belt if required.

Mud: Step four

When the rate of bubble drops to below one per minute, use the hydrometer to check the gravity is beneath 1,008 degrees.


Check again a day or two later to ensure it remains below 1,008 degrees. If stable, syphon the beer into bottles or a secondary fermentation vessel. Be careful not to disturb the sediment and Don't seal the bottles.

Mud: Step five

Add half a teaspoon of sugar to each bottle, or 85g (3oz) to the vessel.


Mix the contents of the vessel and bottle using the bottle filler. Fit the crowns to the bottles and record the date.

Mud: Step six

Stand the bottles in a warm place for about two days, before moving the bottles to the bunker for at least two weeks before drinking.


Enjoy your Mud!
