How to fit an airlock


An airlock lets air created during the fermentation escape from the vessel without letting unwanted air


Fill the airlock with boiled water, close it, and gently but tightly fit it to the top of the fermentation vessel with the use of a grommet.


When bubbles escape the airlock at a rate of less than one per minute, the wort can be transferred to another vessel or into bottles for secondary fermentation.
