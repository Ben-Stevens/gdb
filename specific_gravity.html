How to take a specific gravity reading


Specific gravity readings are used to calculate the ABV of a beer and to know when the beer is ready for the secondary fermentation.


To take a specific gravity reading, take a sterilised measuring cylinder's worth of wort, being careful not to allow contaminants into the brew. Then flot the hydrometer in the cylinder and read the calibration against the miniscus.


To calculate the ABV, take the original gravity reading (OG) before the yeast is added and a final gravity reading (FG) after fermentation. Then use the following calculation.


(OG-FG)x131.25=ABV%
